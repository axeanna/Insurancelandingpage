OVERVIEW OF PRUMILLION MED ACTIVE 2.0 
1. What is PRUMillion Med Active 2.0?  
PRUMillion Med Active 2.0 is a reintroduction of PRUMillion Med Active with an updated Active Pricing 
structure, with no changes to the medical coverage and insurance charges.  
 
It is a comprehensive medical rider that rewards healthy lifestyle and provides a discount on medical 
Insurance Charges when there no claims are made. It offers a high annual limit up to RM8 million with no 
lifetime limit. The rider reimburses medical expenses according to the selected plan in the event of 
hospitalization and for outpatient treatment due to illness or accidental injury. 
2. What are the basic plans that can attach PRUMillion Med Active 2.0?  
PRUMillion Med Active 2.0 is available for attachment as a new medical plan to PRUWith You Plus and other 
applicable investment-linked plans. 
 
Note: Refer to Appendix I for the list of applicable investment-linked plans.

ACTIVE PRICING 
4. What is Active Pricing? 
Active Pricing is a tiered pricing approach where a discount or an additional charge will be applied to the Base 
Level Insurance Charges, subject to the terms and conditions. Customer will receive discount on the Base Level 
Insurance Charges from the effective date of the policy and continue to enjoy it as long as no claim is made. 
Customer will need to pay temporary additional premiums (“Active Premium”) to cover the higher Insurance 
Charges when claims are made and approved, subject to the terms and conditions. 
5. What is Active Premium? 
It is the temporary additional premium required at the next policy anniversary after claim is approved during 
the preceding Review Period. It is charged to cover the higher Insurance Charges when the Insurance Charges 
are at the Base Level or Stack-Up Level (Tier 1 or Tier 2).  
6. Where can agent see the Active Premium amount to illustrate the claim scenarios to the customers? 
Agents may refer to the Active Premium table illustrated in the Product Illustration (PI), Summary of 
Projected Active Premiums table appended under Policy Document and Endorsement Slip, as well as the  
PRUMillion Med Active 2.0 placemat, to view the Active Premiums payable at the next policy anniversary.

payment frequency.

Agents may find the placemat in the PRUForce app (Insurance Product Library -> PRUMillion Med Active

8. Does customer still need to pay for Active Premium under payor status?

Premium (if any). No Active Premium is required when the policy is under payor status. The Insurance Charges

• 
RM20,000 will be paid by Sam; and 
• 
RM280,000 will be paid by PAMB 
•

KEY FEATURES & BENEFITS OF PRUMILLION MED ACTIVE 2.0 
25. What is covered under Organ/Bone Marrow Transplant Benefit and how are organ/bone marrow 
transplants covered under PRUMillion Med Active 2.0?  
Organ/Bone Marrow Transplant Benefit of PRUMillion Med Active 2.0 covers reasonable and customary 
charges incurred for medically necessary transplant of a kidney, heart, lungs, liver or bone marrow on the Life 
Assured as a recipient of the organ or bone marrow. Any costs incurred by the donors or any costs to 
transport, store the organs and the cost to purchase the organs or bone marrow is not payable. This benefit is 
payable once per lifetime. Note that transplants of organs which are not one of the listed organs will not be 
subject to the limit of once per lifetime. 
26. What is covered under Outpatient Cancer Treatment benefit? 
Outpatient Cancer Treatment benefit covers reasonable and customary charges incurred for medically 
necessary outpatient treatment of cancer performed at a registered cancer treatment centre or an outpatient 
department of a hospital. These include take home drugs, examination tests and consultation fees.  
 
We will also reimburse reasonable and customary charges incurred for medically necessary diagnostic test, 
screening test, and consultation follow-up fee pertaining to monitoring of recurrence of cancer, on 
recommendation of the attending doctor. The diagnostic test and screening test performed must be related to 
the cancer type that the Life Assured was diagnosed earlier. This benefit is payable following any cancer 
treatment received by the Life Assured, and will be payable up to 5 years from the cancer remission date.  
 
More information on cancer remission can be found in the FAQ on our website (FAQs about Insurance Claims | 
Prudential Malaysia). 
 
(HAS or cash-less facility will be available for claims on Outpatient Cancer Treatment.) 
27. What is covered under Outpatient Kidney Dialysis Treatment benefit? 
Outpatient Kidney Dialysis Treatment benefit covers reasonable and customary charges incurred for medically 
necessary outpatient treatment of kidney dialysis at the outpatient department of a hospital or a dialysis 
treatment centre. These include take home drugs, examination tests and consultation fees.  
 
(HAS or cash-less facility will be available for claims on Outpatient Kidney Dialysis Treatment.) 
28. What is covered under Outpatient Rehabilitation Treatment Benefit?  
Outpatient Rehabilitation Treatment Benefit covers reasonable and customary charges incurred for the 
following medically necessary treatments. 
 
Physiotherapy Treatment 
• 
Physiotherapy Treatment Benefit covers reasonable and customary charges incurred for medically 
necessary follow-up physiotherapy treatment performed within 180 days after hospital discharge, for the 
medical condition for which hospitalisation was required.  
• 
This shall exclude the medicines prescribed during follow-up physiotherapy treatment. 
• 
Physiotherapy treatment must be recommended by a specialist, and must be treated at the Outpatient 
department of a hospital or a legally registered physiotherapy centre. 
 
 
 
 
Alternative Treatment (applicable for Plan 300 and above only) 
• 
Alternative Treatment Benefit covers reasonable and customary charges incurred for covered outpatient 
Alternative Treatment received within 180 days after hospital discharge, for the medical condition for 
which hospitalisation was required. We shall only cover the following Alternative Treatment: 
- Acupuncture 
- Bonesetting 
- Chiropractic 
- Herbalist Treatment 
- Traditional Chinese Medicine 
- Moxibustion Therapy 
- Acupressure 
- Homeopathy 
- Osteopathy 
• 
The Life Assured must first seek treatment from a doctor prior to seeking treatment from Traditional and 
Complementary Medicine (TCM) Practitioner. 
 
Note: We may require the Life Assured to give us satisfactory proof that the TCM Practitioner is 
registered. 
29. What is covered under Outpatient Illness Treatment Benefit? 
Outpatient Illness Treatment Benefit covers reasonable and customary charges incurred for medically 
necessary treatment of bronchitis, dengue fever, influenza, or pneumonia where the treatment must be 
received at the outpatient department of a hospital or a registered clinic.  
 
We reserve the right to treat any in-patient treatment of above-mentioned illnesses as outpatient illness 
treatment when in our opinion such in-patient treatment could have been done as an outpatient illness 
treatment. If we do so, we shall pay under outpatient illness treatment benefit, and the limit under this 
benefit will be reduced accordingly. 
 
For bronchitis or pneumonia, the diagnosis must be supported by written confirmation from the treating 
doctor together with blood test and diagnostic imaging (X-ray or CT scan) for confirmation of bronchitis or 
pneumonia, where applicable. 
 
For dengue fever or influenza, the diagnosis must be supported by written confirmation from the treating 
doctor together with serology or equivalent test confirmation of dengue fever or influenza, where applicable.  
 
For ease of claiming and to avoid delay in the paying out of claims, please provide all the bills, written 
diagnosis from treating doctor and applicable test results for the illness event together in one claim. Please 
use the latest claims form and indicate the type of benefit accordingly. 
30. What is covered under Home Nursing Care? 
Home Nursing Care benefit covers reasonable and customary charges incurred for continued nursing care by a 
Registered Nurse to the Life Assured in a home within 14 days immediately after the Life Assured’s discharge 
from the Hospital up to 200 days per lifetime. The nursing care must be recommended by the same surgeon or 
doctor as a result of the same disability that the Life Assured was confined to a hospital for. 
31. What is covered under Emergency Treatment for Accidental Injury benefit? 
Emergency Treatment for Accidental Injury Benefit covers emergency outpatient treatment of an accidental 
injury at any clinic or hospital within 48 hours of the accident causing the bodily injury. Any follow-up 
treatment shall not be more than 30 days from the date of the accident.   
 
 
Treatment shall include, but not limited, to the following: 
• 
Closed manipulation / reduction of bone fracture or joint dislocation 
• 
Application of Plaster of Paris  
• 
Removal of foreign body from eye / ear / nose / throat 
• 
Toilet and Suture of laceration wound  
• 
Dressing of injury wound 
 
Emergency Treatment for Accidental Injury Benefit also includes dental treatment of an accidental injury 
provided the treatment is received within 48 hours of the accident by a dentist at any dental clinic or hospital.  
 
Dental treatments shall include replacement/restoration of sound natural teeth necessary for restoration of 
function such as:  
• 
placement of denture  
• 
prosthetic services such as bridges and crowns   
• 
dental implants 
 
The expenses for the following conditions shall not be covered: 
• 
dental appliances  
• 
charges for routine check-up  
• 
charges for replacement of dentures or prosthesis which include charges for replacement of congenitally 
missing teeth or teeth which were lost before the date the accident that caused the said bodily injury, or 
the commencement date of PRUMillion Med Active 2.0, or the date it is revived, whichever is later. 
• 
treatment by someone other than a Dentist 
32. What is Intraocular Lens Benefit? 
Intraocular Lens Benefit reimburses the reasonable and customary charges incurred for medically necessary 
intraocular lenses (either monofocal or multifocal lenses) for cataract or eye injury up to RM8,000 per lifetime. 
33. Can the Life Assured claim more than RM1,000 for multifocal lenses under the Intraocular Lens Benefit? 
Yes, the Life Assured can claim up to RM8,000 per lifetime for either monofocal or multifocal lenses for 
cataract or eye injury. 
34. What is Expert Medical Opinion? 
It is a value-added service that provides second medical opinion from medical experts in various areas of 
specialisation. This service is to assist customers to learn more about their medical conditions and make an 
informed decision on the treatment plans. 
 
We connect customers with the medical experts via an online portal, where they can upload all their medical 
reports and select the related medical specialty (such as cardiology, orthopedic and etc.). After analyzing their 
medical reports, an expert report will be shared with the customers, which allows them to learn more about 
their medical conditions as well as the suitable treatment plan. 
 
Expert Medical Opinion is a service provided from third party provider which is non-contractual in nature and 
does not form part of the policy. We have the right to terminate this service by giving a 90-day written notice. 
 
Customer can refer to www.prudential.com.my for more details on this service.

61. What are the major exclusions and limitations under PRUMillion Med Active 2.0 and Active Booster?  
Except for any claims made under No Claims Benefit, medical benefits are not paid as a consequence of: 
 
(a) pre-existing conditions; 
(b) Specified Illnesses occurring during the first 120 days from the effective date of the rider or the date it is 
revived, whichever is later: 
(1) hypertension, diabetes mellitus and cardiovascular disease; 
(2) growths of any kind including tumours, cancers, cysts, nodules, polyps; 
(3) stones of the urinary system and biliary system; 
(4) any disease of the ear, nose (including sinuses) or throat; 
(5) hernias, haemorrhoids, fistulae, hydrocele or varicocele; 
(6) any disease of the reproductive system including endometriosis; or 
(7) any disorders of the spine (including a slipped disc) and knee conditions; 
 
(c) any medical or physical conditions and its signs or symptoms occurring within the first 30 days from the 
effective date of the rider or the date it is revived, whichever is later, except for traumatic bodily injury 
caused by an accident; 
(d) any neonatal medical or physical conditions including birth trauma (a physical injury sustained by an 
infant during birth) occurring within the first 30 days from the effective date of the rider, the date it is 
revived or the date of birth of the Life Assured, whichever is the latest; 
(e) any benefits as provided under the Maternity Complications Benefit occurring within the first 365 days 
from the effective date of the rider or the date it is revived, whichever is later; 
(f) elective cosmetic or plastic surgery (except re-constructive surgery necessary to restore function), 
hyperhidrosis, circumcision, eye examination for nearsightedness, farsightedness or astigmatism, visual 
aids and refraction or surgical correction of nearsightedness (Radial Keratotomy) and the use or 
acquisition of external prosthetic appliances or devices such as but not limited to artificial limbs, hearing 
aids, cochlear apparatus, external or  temporary pacemakers and prescriptions thereof;  
(g) dental conditions including dental treatment or oral surgery except as necessitated by accident to restore 
function of sound natural teeth occurring while the policy and the rider are in force; 
(h) private nursing (except for Home Nursing Care Benefit), rest cures or sanitaria care; 
(i) drug abuse, addictive disorders from any kind of substance or alcohol use or misuse, under influence of 
alcohol, venereal disease and its sequelae, AIDS (Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome) or ARC (AIDS 
Related Complex) and HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) related diseases; 
(j) any treatment or surgical operation for congenital abnormalities or deformities including hereditary 
conditions;  
(k) pregnancy, child birth (including surgical delivery), miscarriage, abortion and prenatal or postnatal care 
and surgical, mechanical or chemical contraceptive methods of birth control or treatment pertaining to 
infertility and its complications, with exception to the benefits as provided under the Maternity 
Complications Benefit. Erectile dysfunction and tests or treatment related to impotence or sterilization;  
(l) primarily for investigatory purposes, diagnosis, X-ray examination, stem cell therapy, general physical or 
medical examinations, not incidental to treatment or diagnosis of a covered disability or any treatment 
which is not medically necessary and any preventive treatments, preventive medicines or examinations 
carried out by a physician, and treatments specifically for weight reduction or gain or bariatric surgery;  
(m) suicide, attempted suicide or intentionally self-inflicted injury while sane or insane;  
(n) war or any act of war, declared or undeclared, criminal or terrorist activities, act of foreign enemies, 
active duty in any armed forces, direct participation in strikes, riots, civil commotion, insurrection, 
revolution or any war-like operations;

POLICY OPTIONS 
62. Can a person change the benefit of his/her PRUMillion Med Active 2.0 after inception? 
Yes, the policyholder can change the benefits as listed below via endorsement.  
➢ Inclusion / removal of PRUMillion Med Active 2.0; and/or 
➢ Increase / decrease the Hospital Daily Room & Board (200/300/400); and/or 
➢ Increase / decrease the Deductible (500/1,000/5,000/10,000); and/or 
➢ Inclusion / removal of 10% Co-insurance option; and/or 
➢ Change of Date of Deductible 
 
This can be done using the Supplementary Proposal Form (SPF) with additional premium (if required) and 
subject to underwriting for upgrading, or the Alteration Request Form (ARF) for any cancellation or reduction 
of benefits. Changes to the benefits can be performed and submitted via our e-platform, PRUServe Plus. 
Alternatively, the SPF and ARF are also available at branches, PRUPartner and RAISe. 
63. Does the Cancellation From Inception (CFI) clause apply for PRUMillion Med Active 2.0?  
Yes, CFI is applicable. There is a free-look period of 15 days after the delivery of the policy to the customer. If 
the policy is cancelled within this period, the value of units (at next pricing day) plus the unallocated 
premiums, service charge, Insurance Charges, guarantee charges (if any) and administration charges (if any) 
less medical expenses will be refunded. 
64. Is inclusion of Active Booster allowed after policy incepted? [Updated as of 5 Apr 2026] 
Inclusion of Active Booster is no longer allowed after 1 March 2026. 
65. Is cancellation of Active Booster allowed? 
Yes, cancellation of Active Booster is allowed anytime including solely cancel booster rider or cancel both base 
medical plan and booster rider together.  
 
Cancellation of base medical plan will cancel the booster rider automatically. 
 
66. Is upgrade/downgrade Active Booster allowed?   
Yes, it can be done by upgrading/downgrading for PRUMillion Med Active 2.0 because the R&B of Active 
Booster will follow the basic medical card it is attached to. The annexure date will be changed to the 
endorsement issuance date as well. 
 
CLAIMS SCENARIOS 
67. How will the difference between the Hospital Daily Room & Board and the actual room & board charges be 
handled?  
The Hospital Daily Room & Board covers for the Life Assured’s accommodation in the hospital in the event of a 
hospitalisation. We will pay the actual room charges up to the entitled room & board amount (excluding room 
& board while in Intensive Care Unit/Cardiac Care Unit). We will NOT refund the excess room & board if the 
Life Assured chooses to stay in a room that is lower than his/her entitlement during hospitalisation. 
 
Please refer to the scenarios below:- 
 
Customer has the following plan: Room & Board = RM200 per day 
 
Scenario 1: 
Life Assured is hospitalized for 5 days at Room & Board rate of RM250 per day. 
Upon discharge, the following is payable: 
Actual Total Room & Board  
= 5 x RM250 = RM1,250 
Total Room & Board entitlement  
= 5 x RM200 = RM1,000 
Total payable by us to hospital*  
= RM1,000 
Total borne by customer  
= RM250 
 
Scenario 2: 
Life Assured is hospitalized for 5 days at Room & Board rate of RM150 per day. 
Upon discharge, the following is payable: 
Actual Total Room & Board  
= 5 x RM150 = RM750 
Total Room & Board entitlement  
= 5 x RM200 = RM1,000 
Total payable by us to hospital*  
= RM750 
Balance of R&B benefit 
 
 = RM250 (will not be refunded to customer) 
 
*Total claims payable subject to annual limit and Deductible. 
 
68. If a claim can fall under more than 1 Benefit, can the policyholder choose which Benefit clause to claim 
under for PRUMillion Med Active 2.0?  
No. Where a claim(s) may fall under 1 or more Benefits, PRUMillion Med Active 2.0 will cover the claim based 
on the Benefit that has an inner limit, if any. 
 
For example, outpatient physiotherapy treatment may fall under Outpatient Rehabilitation Treatment Benefit 
and Post-Hospitalisation Treatment Benefit. The claim shall be paid under the Outpatient Rehabilitation 
Treatment Benefit only as it prescribes an inner limit.

Medical Plan per Life Rule 
A Life Assured is allowed to own more than 1 medical plan, and there is no maximum on the total number of

76. Is High Dependency Unit (HDU) covered under Hospital Daily Room & Board Benefit or under Intensive Care

77. Are implants and external appliances covered under PRUMillion Med Active 2.0 and Active Booster? 
Medically Necessary implants and external appliances are covered under PRUMillion Med Active 2.0, subject 
to Reasonable and Customary Charges, which ordinarily applied in claims assessment. This is covered under 
the In-Hospital & Related Services Benefit. Please do note that external appliances coverage is limited to usage 
during in-hospital ONLY, and is not for take home. 
 
 
The following are some common items as examples for illustration purposes only and is non-exhaustive: 
• Coverable implants - screws & plates for bone fracture, intraocular lens for cataract, permanent implanted 
pacemaker for heart, etc.  
• Excluded implants - breast implant, cochlear implant for the ear, stem cell implant, etc. 
• Coverable appliances - POP/splint for bone fracture, surgical gloves, oxygen mask, surgical devices, 
nebulizer for asthma, traction device for spine, infusion pump for medicines, holter for irregular heartbeat, 
etc. 
• Excluded appliances - wheel chair, crutches, walking boots/aids, CPAP machines for sleep apnea, surgical 
stocking, aero-chamber for asthma, all prostheses, blood sugar monitoring devices/needles for diabetes, 
etc. 
78. Are claims incurred for General Practitioner consultation covered under Outpatient Treatment Benefits? 
Yes. We will reimburse Reasonable and Customary charges on eligible expenses incurred for General 
Practitioner consultation covered under Outpatient Treatment Benefits which are deemed Medically 
Necessary. 
79. How do I know what is not covered under Medically Necessary? 
You may refer to the “Non-medically Necessary Services” list available at our website or through any other

Necessary”. Do note that the list is not exhaustive and we can change it from time to time without any notice. 
 
80. Will the policyholder receive a physical medical card with their policy?  
Starting from 2nd July 2018, policyholders will no longer receive the physical medical card for all medical plans. 
Policyholders can access their eMedical Card in their PRUServices account. There is no change to the existing

APPENDIX III: DEFINITIONS 
 
1. Specified Illnesses  
Refers to the following Disabilities or any complications caused by such Disabilities occurring within the first 
120 days of commencement date or the date it is revived, whichever is the later: 
(a) Hypertension, diabetes mellitus and Cardiovascular disease; 
(b) Growths of any kind including tumours, cancers, cysts, nodules, polyps; 
(c) Stones of the urinary system and biliary system; 
(d) Any disease of the ear, nose (including sinuses) or throat; 
(e) Hernias, haemorrhoids, fistulae, hydrocele or varicocele; 
(f) Any disease of the reproductive system including endometriosis; or 
(g) Any disorders of the spine (including a slipped disc) and knee conditions. 
 
2. Doctor or Surgeon or Physician 
means a registered medical practitioner qualified and licensed to practice western medicine. In providing 
treatment, they must be practicing within the scope of their licensing and training in the geographical area of 
practice. This person cannot be you, the Life Assured, the Life Assured’s or your husband or wife or a close 
relative. 
 
3. Medically Necessary  
A medical service, which is: 
(a) consistent with the diagnosis and customary medical treatment for a Disability; 
(b) in accordance with standards of good medical practice, consistent with current standard of professional 
medical care, and of proven medical benefits; 
(c) not for the convenience of the Life Assured or the Doctor, and unable to be reasonably rendered out of 
Hospital (if admitted as an inpatient); 
(d) not of an experimental, investigational or research nature, preventive or screening nature;  
(e) for which the charges are fair and reasonable and customary for the Disability; and 
(f) provide treatment directly related to the covered Disability.   
 
You can refer to the “Non-Medically Necessary Services” list available at our website or through any other 
media or electronic means, for examples of medical services that do not satisfy the definition of “Medically 
Necessary” provided. The list is not exhaustive and we can change the list from time to time without any 
notice. 
 
4. Reasonable and Customary Charges 
Charges for medical care which is Medically Necessary shall be considered reasonable and customary to the 
extent that it does not exceed the general level of charges being imposed by other legally registered providers 
of medical or healthcare services of similar standing within Malaysia. 
 
Such charges when incurred, taking into consideration similar or comparable treatment, services or supplies to 
individuals of the same gender and of comparable age of similar sickness, disease or injury and in accordance 
with accepted medical standards and practice could not have been omitted without adversely affecting the 
Life Assured’s medical condition.
