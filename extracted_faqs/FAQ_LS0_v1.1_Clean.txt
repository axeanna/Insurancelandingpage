OVERVIEW OF LEGACY SETTLEMENT OPTION (LSO) 
 
1. What is the Legacy Settlement Option (LSO)?  
Legacy Settlement Option is a facility that gives you (as a Policyowner) the flexibility to structure how 
(amount, timing, method) the benefit payout to nominees upon death of Life Assured, whether as a 
lump sum, regular instalments (up to 35 years), or milestone-based payouts.  
 
This option helps ensure your loved ones receive support in a way that aligns with your wishes, creating 
a purpose-driven legacy and meaningful wealth distribution. 
 
2. What are the options available under LSO? 
The Policyowner (also knowns as the Assured) can customise the death benefit payout with the options 
below:  
Payout Options 
Description 
Immediate Lum 
Sum Payout 
Upon death of the Life Assured (also the Policyowner), the moneys payable 
under this Policy will be payable in a lump sum to the nominee(s) in the 
respective proportions based on the nomination made by the Policyowner. 
Structured 
Instalment 
Payout 
Upon death of the Life Assured (also the Policyowner), the moneys payable 
under this Policy will be payable to the nominee(s) in the respective 
proportions based on the nomination made by the Assured/Policyowner, in 
monthly or yearly instalments, ranging from a minimum of 5 years to a 
maximum of 35 years.

based Payout

be payable to the nominee(s) in the respective proportions based on the 
nomination made by the Policyowner, when the nominee(s) completes the 
life event(s) as determined by the Policyowner. 
 
Policyowner may choose up to three (3) life events out of the life events 
for each nominee as may be set by PAMB in the application form.

4. What are the eligibility criteria to apply for LSO? 
To apply for the LSO, these conditions must be met: 
• 
The policy must be first-party policies (Policyowner and Life Assured are the same person) are 
eligible. 
• 
There must be valid nomination in place. 
• 
Basic Sum Assured is at least RM1 million. 
• 
The policy must not have any prior assignment. 
 
5. What is the minimum setting for the Payout Options under LSO 
• 
Allocations must be entered in percentages (%), not amounts.  
• 
Each allocation should be in whole-percent increments (multiples of 1%).  
• 
The total allocation across all selected options for all Nominee(s) must sum to 100%. 
 
6. When can the Policyowner apply for LSO? 
You can apply for the LSO at the point of policy purchase or anytime during the policy term, as long 
as the eligibility criteria are met. 
 
This gives you flexibility to plan your legacy when it matters most.

8. Can Policyowner make changes to the payout structure later? 
Yes, Policyowner can revise or remove the payout structure after opting for LSO, provided the request 
is made during the policy term and while the Life Assured Life Assured (also the Policyowner)  is still 
alive, by submitting the “Trust and Nomination / Legacy Settlement Option Form”. 
 
This ensures your legacy plan stays aligned with your evolving wishes. 
 
9. Can Nominee(s) make changes to the payout structure later? 
No. Only the Policyowner can make changes to the LSO arrangement.

10. Is trustee consent required for changes to the Legacy Settlement Option (LSO) arrangement? 
Yes. Similar to the current practice for updating nominations, any change to the LSO arrangement—
such as adjusting proportions, payout options, payout term, or milestone events—requires trustee 
consent.  
  
However, changing the personalized message for milestone-based payouts does not require trustee 
consent. 
Reference: Financial Service Act 2013, Schedule 10 
Subparagraph 5(5)

12. Is there any cost to apply for LSO? 
No, there is no additional cost to apply for the LSO. It is a value-added facility to help you plan a 
meaningful and structured legacy for your loved ones. 
 
13. How will Nominee(s) be notified about the arrangement made by the Policyowner through LSO? 
Without Appointed Trustee 
• 
After the death claim is confirmed, each Nominee receives a separate Death Claim Letter detailing 
their own payout arrangement under LSO, together with any customised message under 
Milestone-based Payout. (see Q39)  
• 
Each Nominee is only informed of their own portion. 
 
With Appointed Trustee 
• 
After the death claim is confirmed, a Death Claim Letter is sent to the Appointed Trustee, detailing 
payout arrangement for the Nominee(s) under LSO, together with any customised message under 
Milestone-based Payout. (see Q39) 
• 
The Trustee will manage and distribute payouts according to the LSO instructions for all Nominees. 
 
14. What would cause the Payout Options under LSO be void from the date of approval of the 
application?  
(a) If the policy moneys payable under the policy with LSO is below Ringgit Malaysia Five Hundred 
Thousand (RM500,000.00) upon the death of the Life Assured; 
(b) A new Trust & Nomination Form is submitted to us and approved by us;  
(c) There is any change in nomination for this Policy and LSO was not selected for the updated 
nomination; 
(d) This Policy is assigned or novated; 
(e) There is false information provided or false declaration made during the application for LSO; or 
(f) The policy with LSO is terminated, avoided or cancelled. 
 
If any of the clause 11(a) to 11(e) happens, the moneys payable under this policy shall be payable in a 
lump sum to the nominee(s) in the respective proportions based on the nomination made by the 
Policyowner.

16. What happens if the Trustee passes away? 
One (1) Trustee is appointed 
If the appointed/presumed/auto Trustee dies or cannot act, and no replacement is appointed, the 
policy money (proceeds) will be payable in the following sequence: 
(a) Nominee who is competent to contract; or 
(b) Parent of the incompetent Nominee; or 
(c) Public Trustee 
Reference: Financial Service Act 2013, Schedule 10 
Subparagraph 5(3) 
 
 
 
Multiple Trustees are appointed 
The surviving Appointed Trustee(s) will continue as the sole Trustee and upon death of Life Assured, 
will receive the claim notification and administer/distribute the policy moneys (proceed) to the 
respective Nominee(s) according to the nomination (and LSO, if any). 
 
17. What would cause the Payout Options under LSO be void from the date of approval of the 
application?  
In Malaysia a valid insurance trust nomination is generally not contestable unless it is proved that the 
policy was affected and premiums paid with intent to defraud a creditor of the policy owner.

NON-GUARANTEED INTEREST:  
 
45. What is the non‑guaranteed interest under LSO? 
The non‑guaranteed interest refers to interest that may be accrued on the remaining (unpaid) balance 
of policy moneys after a death claim has been approved by Prudential, where the policy money is paid 
out progressively under the LSO. 
 
46. When does the Non‑Guaranteed Interest start to apply? 
The non‑guaranteed interest applies only after the death claim has been approved by Prudential and 
only if there is an remaining (unpaid) balance of policy moneys under LSO. 
 
47. Is interest applied to the remaining (unpaid) policy moneys?  
Yes, interest may be accrued on the unpaid balance of policy moneys and compounded on a monthly 
basis, in accordance with the prevailing non-guaranteed interest rate.  
 
48. What is the interest rate? 
The interest rate is non-guaranteed, compounded monthly, and currently 3% p.a., effective from 11 
January 2026 until further notice. (The rate is determined by us and may vary from time to time) 
 
49. Is the interest rate guaranteed? 
No. The interest rate is non‑guaranteed. 
 
50. On what basis will the interest rate change? 
The rate is reviewed periodically based on overall investment performance of underlying assets, 
primarily fixed‑income instruments (e.g., bonds). Returns generally align with market interest rates 
(e.g., Overnight Policy Rate) with smoothing techniques to help minimize short‑term fluctuations.  
 
51. Where can customers check the applicable or historical interest rates? 
Historical interest rates will be published on our corporate website under the Legacy Settlement 
Option page. 
 
52. How and when will the interest accrued be payable?  
Any interest accrued under LSO will be paid together with the final payout to each Nominee.  
 
 
13 
 
53. Will the non‑guaranteed interest be illustrated in the Product Illustration and Product Disclosure 
Sheet (PIPDS)?  
No, The non‑guaranteed interest under LSO will not be illustrated in the PIPDS because the interest 
amount is contingent on several post‑death factors that cannot be determined at the point of sale, 
including but not limited to: 
• 
The timing of the death claim approval and settlement; 
• 
The LSO payout structure and allocation; 
• 
The unpaid balance of the death benefit when LSO commences; 
• 
The timing of life event completion; 
• 
The instalment payouts term and frequency; and/or 
• 
The prevailing non-guaranteed interest rate at the relevant time. 
PAYMENT TO NOMINEE(S) WHO IS COMPETENT TO CONTRACT:  
54. What happens if the appointed trustee does not wish to manage the policy payout for an extended 
period? 
To address this situation, we offer the “Payment to Nominee(s) Who is Competent to Contract” option. 
This allows policy money to be paid directly to a nominee once they become legally competent to 
contract (for example, upon reaching age 18 and being of sound mind), instead of continuing under 
Appointed Trustee’s management. 
 
 
55. What does “competent to contract” mean?  
As defined under the Financial Services Act 2013, a Nominee is competent to contract if he/she: 
• 
has attained age 18; and 
• 
is of sound mind at the time of payment. 
 
56. What are the requirements for this payout option? 
• 
All Nominees are under age 18 at the point of nomination. 
• 
Trustee(s) is/are appointed. 
• 
Each Nominee’s relationship to the Policyowner is child. 
 
57. Will policy moneys automatically be paid to a Nominee who becomes competent to contract? 
No. The Trustee and/or Nominee must submit a request to Prudential, together with the Nominee’s 
identity document (IC or passport) and a declaration confirming sound mind at the time of payment. 
 
58. What if the Nominee does not inform Prudential? 
Policy money will be paid to the Appointed Trustee(s). 
The receipt of a trustee shall discharge the insurer from all liability for the policy money paid to the 
trustee. 
 
 
14 
 
59. How can the Policyowner apply for this option? 
New Business 
Tick the “PAYMENT TO NOMINEE(S) WHO IS COMPETENT TO CONTRACT” option under E-Signature in 
the proposal form and submit via PRUWay Plus (PWP). 
 
Policy Servicing 
Tick and submit the same option in the Trust and Nomination / Legacy Settlement Option Form (before 
the Trustee signature section). Form will be available on the corporate website and RAISe. 
 
 
 
60. Is consent from the Appointed Trustee(s) required? 
Yes. Both the Appointed Trustee(s) and the Policyowner must agree to this payout option before 
ticking consent. 
 
61. Is LSO required to opt for this option (Payment to Nominee(s) Who is Competent to Contract)? 
No. LSO is not compulsory for this option. The “Payment to Nominee(s) Who is Competent to Contract” 
feature can be selected independently, even if the Policyowner does not opt for LSO.
