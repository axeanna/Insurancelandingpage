OVERVIEW OF PRUWITH YOU 
 
 
 
 
7
1. What is PRUWith You Plus? 
PRUWith You Plus is a flexible, comprehensive and competitive investment-linked protection solution for all 
Malaysians. PRUWith You Plus provides basic protection for death and Total and Permanent Disability (TPD), as well 
as other comprehensive coverage through optional riders that fulfil the needs of the customer at all life stages. 
 
Please refer to Appendix A for the detail of insurance solutions introduced. 
 
2. Who are the target customers?  
Any individual who is looking for a comprehensive investment-linked insurance plan which includes medical, 
comprehensive critical illness coverage etc.  
 
3. Who are the non-target customers? 
Any individual who is looking for guaranteed returns and pure critical illness plan/ medical plan/ pure savings plan/ 
pure life insurance plan. 
 
4. What are the enhancements in PRUWith You Plus compared to PRUWith You? 
• Sum Assured Booster: Additional 1% of Basic Sum Assured (BSA) every year1 at no extra cost, up to maximum 
of 50% of BSA in the event of death, TPD2 or attaining the Policy Anniversary of Life Assured’s ANB 101. 
• Child Benefit: Additional 30% of basic sum assured for 
a) death of Life Assured’s child(ren) (exclude the death of foetus); and/or 
b) Life Assured’s child(ren) is diagnosed of a covered child specified Illness, on or prior to attaining 7 years 
old or expiry of the policy, whichever is earlier. 
• New rider - Income Benefit: 100% of rider sum assured be credited to Investment Unit Account (IUA) annually 
upon Life Assured suffering from TPD or diagnosis of a covered critical illness. 
 
Notes:  
1Starting from 2nd policy year. 
2The Sum Assured Booster for TPD is up to 50% of the TPD Sum Assured. 
 
 
POLICY TERM & ELIGIBILITY 
 
5. Who is eligible for PRUWith You Plus? 
Foetus from 13 gestational weeks to individual aged 70 next birthday are eligible for PRUWith You Plus, subject to 
underwriting approval. Please refer to Appendix B for the eligible entry age of each rider. 
 
6. What is the coverage term for PRUWith You Plus? 
This plan covers up to policy anniversary of Life Assured’s ANB 101 as long as there is sufficient cash value to be 
deducted for the charges due. To ensure that the policy has sufficient cash value, additional premium may be required 
for continuous coverage up to ANB 101. Please refer to Appendix B for the coverage term of each rider.

The expected rate of investment return will be reviewed on annual basis. The expected rate of investment return 
assumptions are principally driven by assumptions and future expectations of economic and capital market 
performance over a long term period of 10 years and above. Some of the metrics considered in determining the 
expected rate of investment return include: 
• 
GDP growth 
• 
Inflation rate 
• 
Equity risk premiums 
• 
Market volatility 
• 
Risk-free rate of return (eg. Government bond yield) and 
• 
Cash yield 
The expected rate of investment return will be different from historical or short-term investment returns that are 
impacted by cyclical and temporary market factors, such as impact of Covid-19 in year 2020. On a long-term basis, 
investment returns are expected to move towards the long-term equilibrium level.

36. What are Structural Congenital Conditions? 
Structural Congenital Conditions are birth defects that affect the normal physical development of body parts or organs. Examples 
include but not limited to: 
• 
Cleft Lip; 
• 
abnormal limbs (e.g. Club Foot); 
• 
heart defects (e.g. Atrial/Ventricular Septal Defects, Patent Ductus Arteriosus);   
• 
gastrointestinal defect (e.g. Oesophageal Atresia, Diaphragmatic Hernia, Pyloric Stenosis). 
 
Structural Congenital Conditions excludes Functional birth defects (other than the Congenital Conditions listed above) which are 
related to a problem with how body system works. Examples include but not limited to:  
• 
nervous system and brain problems (e.g. learning disabilities, intellectual impairment, behavioural disorders); 
• 
immune system disorders in which the child’s immune system does not function adequately (e.g. autoimmune diseases); 
• 
neurodevelopmental disorders where the child seems to be developing normally but suddenly starts to lose function and 
previously acquired skills (e.g. Rett Syndrome, Muscular Dystrophy); 
• 
metabolic disorders (e.g. Phenylketonuria, Type 1 Diabetes).

one is diagnosed with subsequent Cancer, Heart Attack and Stroke before age 85, when it is unrelated to the first 
diagnosis. This means that rider covers up to 200% of the rider Sum Assured for critical illness category of Cancer, 
Heart Attack and Stroke. 
 
Additional Coverage for Special Benefit

72. What is PRUHealth? 
PRUHealth is a medical plan that not only pays for hospital bills when needed but also rewards the customer a yearly 
bonus when no claims are paid under this plan for the previous year.  
 
This plan also provides the customer with the option to choose  co-insurance or Med Saver RM 3,000. 
73. What is the difference between coinsurance and Med Saver RM 3,000? 
Coinsurance is a cost-sharing arrangement under which the policyholder pays a specified percentage of the cost of 
an eligible benefit. Under the PRUHealth plan, if coinsurance is selected, policyholder will have to pay 10% of cost of 
an eligible benefit, subject to minimum coinsurance amount of RM 300 and maximum coinsurance amount of 
RM1,000 for Hospital & Surgical Benefit (excluding cost of daily room & board), and maximum coinsurance amount 
of RM 2,000 for Outpatient Treatment Benefit. The remaining balance is paid by the Company up to the annual and 
lifetime limit. 
 
Med Saver on the other hand, is a fixed amount the policyholder must first pay regardless of the total cost of an 
eligible benefit (excluding cost of daily room & board) for any one disability during a 90-day period. If Med Saver of 
RM 3,000 is selected, policyholder will have to pay the first RM 3,000 of the total cost of an eligible benefit (excluding 
cost of daily room & board). The remaining balance is paid by us up to annual and lifetime limit.

Protect Booster 
104. What is Protect Booster? 
Protect Booster is an optional unit deducting rider that is designed to provide a defined term additional lump sum 
benefit upon death of the Life Assured prior to the expiry of the rider. It will not accelerate the BSA.  
105. What is the benefit term for Protect Booster? 
Customer can choose to have additional death benefit coverage for 5, 10, 20 or 30 years. The rider term cannot be 
longer than the policy term. 
106. Will the premium and COI be reduced when Protect Booster expires? 
Premium will remain unchanged and no COI will be charged for Protect Booster after the rider expires. 
107. Who are the target customers?  
The target customers for this rider are young entrepreneurs and young adults who seek for additional death coverage 
within a defined term. 
108. Who are the non-target customers? 
This rider is not suitable for customers who are looking for comprehensive protection such as critical illness coverage, 
medical and accidental benefits or insurance plan with expected returns (guaranteed or non-guaranteed). 
109. Is the insurance charge guaranteed? 
Yes, the rates of insurance charge are guaranteed. 
110. What is the sum assured limit for this rider?

CROSS-SUBSIDY 
131. What is cross-subsidy and what are the benefits? 
Cross-subsidy is the movement of units between accounts to deduct for charges when insufficiency happens in any 
account. When BUA runs out of units to deduct for charges, units in IUA will be used to support those charges. Cross-
subsidy helps to ensure that all benefits continue to be in force at the same time. 
 
 
 
43
132. How does cross-subsidy function for PRUWith You Plus? 
For PRUWith You Plus, when No-Lapse Provision is still in effect, no cross-subsidy from IUA will be triggered. Else, 
when the units in BUA are exhausted, cross-subsidy will be triggered. This is to ensure that there are enough units to 
support the insurance charges and service charge required.  
 
Please refer to Q136-138 for detail on No-Lapse Provision. 
 
 
LAPSE AND REVIVAL 
133. When will the policy be considered lapsed? 
The policy will not lapse if the No-Lapse Provision is still in force. Otherwise, the policy will be considered lapsed when 
there are insufficient values in the unit accounts to cover the insurance charges and service charges incurred. The 
policy however, will be automatically revived if the premium is received within 38 days from lapsed date. 
134. What is No-Lapse Provision? 
The policy is guaranteed to be in force within the first 6 years (72 months) for non-prenatal case / first 8 years (96 
months) for prenatal case regardless the values in the unit accounts, provided all premiums due are paid and no 
withdrawal from BUA is performed.  
 
Any unpaid insurance charge and service charge during the period when No-Lapse Provision is in effect are regarded 
as monies owing to us. We shall deduct the outstanding charges or part of the outstanding charges: 
i. 
by cancelling units in BUA account when there are available units in BUA; 
ii. 
from any benefits or monies payable under the policy; 
iii. 
from any surrender value payable under this policy; or 
iv. 
from any amount payable because of withdrawal from the accounts of the policy. 
135. Is there any terms and conditions applicable to No-Lapse Provision? 
No-lapse provision will be revoked if: 
(a) Premium is not paid up to date; or 
(b) Withdrawal has been made from the BUA. 
Subsequently, the policy shall lapse if there are insufficient units to cover for the charges. 
136. What will happen after No-Lapse Provision period? 
If the total values of units of the BUA and IUA fall below zero after the 6th policy years (or the 73rd month onwards) 
for non-prenatal case / 8th policy years (or the 97th month onwards) for prenatal case, the policy will lapse.  
137. How can a lapsed policy be revived? 
At any time not later than 3 years after the policy has lapsed, we may reinstate the policy subject to satisfactory 
evidence of health free of expense to us, and the payment of at least 3 months premium with a revival application 
form.

Please refer to the marketing materials or policy document for the maximum coverage term for the respective 
benefits. For example, Total and Permanent Disability (TPD) benefit is covered up to Policy Anniversary of Life 
Assured’s age 71 next birthday. In this case, TPD benefit shall end at age 71 next birthday instead of 101 during the

162. Can customer choose a shorter extension term for the policy instead of auto-extension up to ANB 101? 
No. Once the Extension of Coverage Term takes effect, the coverage will continue up to the Policy Anniversary of 
Life Assured's age 101 next birthday or the maximum coverage term of the benefits, whichever is earlier.

surrender, the cash value (if any) in the policy shall be payable.

163. How much does a customer need to pay to sustain the policy until the end of extended term? 
Customer is advised to monitor the policy from time to time. The recommended top-up amount to sustain up to 
the end of the extended term is advised in Annual Statement, as well as in the Policy Alteration Sustainability 
Statement (PASS) whenever an alteration is made. 
 
We will advise the additional premium amount required to sustain up to the end of extended term at least 90 days 
before the start of the extended term. Without customer’s agreement to pay the new premium amount, the 
existing premium payment amount will remain. If the cash value is no longer enough to cover any further 
deductions, the policy shall lapse, and coverage will end. 
164. Are the top-up amounts recommended for the policy to sustain until the end of extension period guaranteed? 
The recommended top-up amounts shown in the Product Disclosure Sheet (PDS), PASS or Annual Statement are 
not guaranteed. These amounts may vary in future depending on the actual investment return, premium payment, 
policy benefits and charges.  Customer may refer to the Annual Statement for the recommended top-up amount 
every year to understand the sustainability evaluation of the policy and how much to top-up in order to improve 
policy sustainability up to the extended term. 
165. Can customers choose to pay the additional top-up amount before the extended term? 
Yes. Customers can pay the additional top-up amount at any time. It is also encouraged to pay the regular premium 
top-up amount in earlier years or from time to time to improve the policy sustainability. 
166. What if a customer does not wish to pay the recommended top-up amount? 
While it is encouraged to perform premium top-up, it is still entirely optional. However, please note that the 
additional top-up amount is recommended to improve the policy sustainability. Performing top-up from time to 
time will help to ensure sufficient cash value for the deduction of the relevant charges, fees and taxes. The policy 
might lapse earlier than expected if there is insufficient cash value to bear the cost of any further deduction.   
167. Can a customer choose to disable the Extension of Coverage Term feature?  
Yes. Customer can request to disable at any time after the policy is issued and prior to the Extension of Coverage

representatives or any Prudential branches: 
• A completed Supplementary Proposal Form (SPF) or Alteration Request Form (ARF) 
• Acknowledged Policy Alteration Sustainability Statement (PASS) 
• Quotation Slip 
In such cases, the coverage will end at the original expiry date of the policy without the extension.

re-enable for this feature?

APPENDIX E: EXAMPLES OF STRUCTURAL CONGENITAL CONDITIONS & FUNCTIONAL BIRTH DEFECTS [UPDATED ON 18 JUNE 
2025] 
 
More examples of Structural Congenital Conditions, include but not limited to: 
• 
Anophthalmia/microphthalmia  
• 
Anotia/microtia  
• 
Gastroschisis 
• 
Absence of kidney, extra ureter, ureterocele, hypospadias 
• 
Intestinal hernia 
• 
Tongue tie  
• 
Hirchsprung’s Disease 
 
*Notes: Only the Structural Congenital Conditions that are severe and require hospital admission will be eligible for a claim. 
 
More examples of Structural Congenital Conditions that do not require hospital admission (not covered), include but not limited to:  
• 
Tongue-Tie (Ankyloglossia): This condition can be treated with a minor outpatient procedure called a frenotomy, in which a 
doctor quickly snips the frenulum to release the tongue and improve mobility.  
• 
Congenital Hip Dysplasia: Often managed without hospitalization, this condition in infants can be effectively treated with 
harnesses or braces that guide proper hip joint development.  
• 
Clubfoot (Congenital Talipes Equinovarus): Typically addressed through non-surgical outpatient methods such as casting, bracing, 
and physical therapy, which help correct the foot’s position over time. 
 
More examples of Functional birth defects (not covered), include but not limited to:  
• 
Wilson’s disease 
• 
Inherited bleeding disorders e.g. Haemophilia
