OVERVIEW OF PRUWEALTH ENRICH 2.0 
 
1. What is PRUWealth Enrich 2.0?  
PRUWealth Enrich 2.0 is a regular premium investment-linked insurance plan with flexible premium 
payment term, and policy term. PRUWealth Enrich 2.0 offers a combination of insurance protection 
and investment. Customers can purchase PRUWealth Enrich 2.0 as early as 14 days old to age 70 next 
birthday. PRUWealth Enrich 2.0 provides high basic life coverage on Death, Total and Permanent 
Disability (TPD) and Accidental Death.  
 
Other than this, PRUWealth Enrich 2.0 also offers Cover Booster, an additional sum assured payable 
in the event of Death or TPD, with no additional insurance charges. PRUWealth Enrich 2.0 also offers 
Loyalty Bonus and Loyalty Booster to reward customers with a percentage of sum assured to be 
credited into Basic Unit Account (BUA), subject to terms and conditions. Other comprehensive 
coverages are also offered through optional riders that fulfill customer’s needs for both protection and 
investment. For example, optional riders that provide critical illnesses benefit, additional death benefit, 
payor benefits and investment opportunities.  
 
PRUWealth Enrich 2.0 further offers the Legacy Settlement Option, allowing you to personalise wealth 
distribution for your loved ones, and the enhanced Total Multi Crisis Care (TMCC) for comprehensive 
critical illness protection with multiple claims.   
  
2. Who are the target customers?  
Customers who are looking for legacy insurance plan with high protection with at least Basic Sum 
Assured (BSA) of RM 300,000 and above.

4. What are the enhancements in PRUWealth Enrich 2.0 compared to PRUWealth Enrich? 
PRUWealth Enrich 2.0 introduces some enhancements: 
• 
Legacy Settlement Option: New enhancement which provides flexibility for estate planning by 
allowing policy proceeds to be settled in a way that supports smooth wealth transfer to 
nominee(s).  This Legacy Settlement Option is the first of its kind in the market. With this option, 
the policyholder have the power to personalise his/her legacy to match the needs of nominee(s), 
whether it’s for immediate financial security, ongoing support, or milestone-based disbursement. 
• 
Enhanced Accidental Death Benefit: In addition to existing coverage, PRUWealth Enrich 2.0 adds 
an extra 400% of Basic Sum Assured (BSA) if accidental death occurs while commuting in a 
commercial aircraft as a fare-paying passenger. 
• 
Total Multi Crisis Care (TMCC): Offers comprehensive protection against multiple critical illness 
events under one rider, enhancing financial security beyond the base plan.

8. How many types of premiums in PRUWealth Enrich 2.0? 
There are total of 3 types of premium in PRUWealth Enrich 2.0, which is Insurance Premium, 
PRUAllocator Premium and Investment Premium. Insurance Premium and PRUAllocator Premium are 
attributed to BUA for insurance coverage while Investment Premium is attributed to IUA for 
investment purpose. 
 
i. 
Insurance Premium is the premium for Basic Benefits, Other Benefits and Riders (if any). 
ii. 
PRUAllocator Premium is the premium for PRUAllocator (if any) with 95% allocation rate in BUA. 
This premium may be automatically attached to the policy. It is also optional to increase if 
customer would like to maximise the PRUAllocator Premium (up to 50% of maximum Insurance 
Premium), after maximising the Insurance Premium. 
iii. Investment Premium is the premium for Investment Unit Account Riders (if any) such as PRUSaver 
or PRUSaver Kid, this is mainly for investment purpose.

11. What are the frequency and methods of payment for PRUWealth Enrich 2.0? 
Premiums can be paid yearly, half-yearly, quarterly, or monthly through direct debit, cash/cheque, or 
credit/debit card.   
 
12. Is the policy guaranteed to be in force if premium is paid up to date? 
The policy is guaranteed to be kept inforce for the first 6 policy years (72 months) as long as all 
premiums due are paid AND no partial withdrawal is made from BUA. 
 
After the 72 months, policy coverage is subject to the policy having sufficient account value to cover 
all the policy charges such as insurance charges, other fees and charges which are deducted from the 
account value. Policy will lapse if the policy has insufficient cash value to cover all the policy charges. 
 
13. Is PRUWealth Enrich 2.0’s premium guaranteed?  
PRUWealth Enrich 2.0 premium is not guaranteed and the company reserves the right to revise the 
premium by giving 3 months’ written notice prior to the revision to be effective.  
 
14. Is advance premium payment allowed for PRUWealth Enrich 2.0?  
Yes. Advance premium is acceptable. When advance premium is paid, Next Premium Due Date will be 
moved forward to a future date depending on customer’s selected premium payment frequency. This 
amount of premium will be unitized immediately at the next available unit pricing. Therefore, no 
discount on the advance premium paid. We reserve the right to revise the mechanism and terms and 
conditions of advance premium payment facility.  
 
Payment of total commission and other compensation (if any) will be payable to agents according to 
the original scheduled premium due date. 
 
15. Can customer get back his/her premium paid upon cancellation of policy?  
Customer can cancel the policy within the 15 days free-look period from the date of delivery of the 
policy and all value of units (if any) at the next valuation date, plus unallocated premiums (if any), plus

19. Under what circumstances will PRUAllocator be attached and why? 
PRUAllocator may be attached under the 2 scenarios below: 
 
Scenario 1: 
PRUAllocator will be automatically attached to comply with the Minimum Sum Assured Multiple (SAM) 
by BNM, if the annual premium required for the BSA exceeds the amount allowed in the guidelines, 
the excess of annual premium will be allocated into PRUAllocator. This usually happens when shorter 
premium payment term is selected. The PRUAllocator that is automatically attached cannot be 
removed or reduced, it may be lower or higher than 50% of maximum Insurance Premium. 
 
If the annual premium does not exceed the amount allowed in the guidelines, PRUAllocator will not 
be attached.

Example 1: 
A male, non smoker with ANB 30 purchases PRUWealth Enrich 2.0 with BSA RM1mil, Policy Term age 
101 and 100% PRULink Managed Fund II.   
 
If he chooses to pay premiums for the full policy term, the annual premium is RM 5,640 which is less 
than the maximum allowable by BNM. Hence all the premiums will be allocated as Insurance Premium 
and PRUAllocator will not be attached. 
If he chooses to pay premiums for 5 years, the annual premium is RM 24,900 which is more than the 
maximum allowable by BNM. Hence, we will allocate the maximum allowable premium (RM 19,992) 
as Insurance Premium and the balance of RM 4,908 as PRUAllocator Premium. 
 
However, customer has the flexibility to pay more for annual premium, by increasing the PRUAllocator 
Premium, up to 50% of maximum Insurance Premium (refer to Scenario 2).  
 
Note: The Life Assured’s date of birth is 31 January 1997, with the policy commencement date on 1 
February 2026.  
 
Example 2: 
A male, non smoker with ANB 50 purchases PRUWealth Enrich 2.0 with BSA RM1mil, Policy Term age 
101 and 100% PRULink Bond Fund II.   
 
If he chooses to pay premiums for the full policy term, the annual premium is RM 18,192 which is less 
than the maximum allowable by BNM. Hence all the premiums will be allocated as Insurance Premium 
and PRUAllocator will not be attached. 
If he chooses to pay premiums for 5 years, the annual premium is RM 89,172 which is more than the 
maximum allowable by BNM. Hence, we will allocate the maximum allowable premium (RM 39,996) 
as Insurance Premium and the balance of RM 49,176 as PRUAllocator Premium.  
 
Please note that in actual circumstances, the premium allocated as Insurance Premium may be slightly 
lesser than the maximum allowable by BNM due to rounding in the system. 
 
Note: The Life Assured’s date of birth is on 31 January 1977, with the policy commencement date on 1 
February 2026.

Storm (wind, rain, snow, sleet, hail, lightning, dust or sand), earthquake, flood, volcanic eruption,

34. What types of public conveyance are covered under Accidental Death Benefit? 
A mode of licensed transport available to the general public that serves to carry its fare paying 
passengers from one place to another on scheduled trips over established routes as its primary 
purpose.  
 
For example, public conveyance includes any public bus, taxi, airport limousine, train, monorail, 
commercial aircraft, ferry and e-hailing services, such as Grab, provided the service is used to transport 
fare paying passengers. 
 
With the exception of cruise liners, this definition does not include private flights and other 
transportation used for leisure and entertainment.

ensure policyholders continue to be covered/protected in the long run.

on their affordability. Minimum and maximum premium of the policy will be determined by the TSO

52. What affects the policy sustainability? 
Cash value in BUA and IUA (if any) determines the policy sustainability. Hence, premium amount, 
regularity of premium payment, insurance charges, fund performance, partial withdrawal (if any) and 
all other charges applicable to the policy will also affect policy sustainability. 
53. What can be done for the policy to sustain longer? 
Customers may improve their policy sustainability by paying higher BUA Premium, by increasing 
PRUAllocator up to maximum allowable, after maximising the Insurance Premium. Customers may also 
consider including regular Investment Premium via PRUSaver / PRUSaver Kid or by performing ad-hoc 
top-up.

55. What is Extension of Coverage Term and how does it work?  
With this feature, the coverage shall be automatically extended to the Policy Anniversary of Life 
Assured's age 101 next birthday or the maximum coverage term of the benefits (whichever is earlier), 
as long as it has sufficient value of units to be deducted to pay for the relevant charges, fees and taxes 
during the extended term.   
 
Additional premium may be required to ensure the sufficiency of cash value for the deduction and all 
the terms and conditions of the policy shall continue to apply during the extended term. 
 
56. Does Extension of Coverage Term applicable to the basic plan only or including rider(s)? 
The Extension of Coverage Term will extend the term of basic plan and attached riders up to the Policy 
Anniversary of the Life Assured’s age 101 next birthday or the maximum coverage term of the benefits, 
whichever is earlier, as long as there is sufficient cash value. 
 
57. What is the maximum coverage term of benefits referring to? 
Please refer to the marketing materials or policy document for the maximum coverage term for the 
respective benefits. For example, Total and Permanent Disability (TPD) benefit is covered up to Policy 
Anniversary of Life Assured’s age 71 next birthday. In this case, TPD benefit shall end at age 71 instead 
of 101 during the extended term. 
 
58. Can customer choose a shorter extension term for the policy instead of auto-extension up to age 
100? 
No. Once the Extension of Coverage Term takes effect, the coverage will continue up to the Policy 
Anniversary of Life Assured's age 101 next birthday or the maximum coverage term of the benefits, 
whichever is earlier. 
 
However, the customer can choose to surrender the policy at any time based on their protection 
needs. Upon surrender, the cash value (if any) in the policy shall be payable. 
 
59. How much does a customer need to pay to sustain the policy until the end of extended term? 
Customer is advised to monitor the policy from time to time. The recommended top-up amount to 
sustain up to the end of the extended term is advised in Annual Statement, as well as in the Policy 
Alteration Sustainability Statement (PASS) whenever an alteration is made. 
 
We will advise the additional premium amount required to sustain up to the end of extended term at 
least 90 days before the start of the extended term. Without customer’s agreement to pay the new 
premium amount, the existing premium payment amount will remain. If the cash value is no longer 
enough to cover any further deductions, the policy shall lapse, and coverage will end. 
 
 
34 
 
60. Are the top-up amounts recommended for the policy to sustain until the end of extension period 
guaranteed? 
The recommended top-up amounts shown in the Product Disclosure Sheet (PDS), PASS or Annual 
Statement are not guaranteed.. Customer may refer to the Annual Statement for the recommended 
top-up amount every year to understand the sustainability evaluation of the policy and how much to 
top-up in order to improve policy sustainability up to the extended term. 
 
61. Can customers choose to pay the additional top-up amount before the extended term? 
Yes. Customers can pay the additional top-up amount at any time. It is also encouraged to pay the 
regular premium top-up amount in earlier years or from time to time to improve the policy 
sustainability. 
 
62. What if a customer does not wish to pay the recommended top-up amount? 
While it is encouraged to perform premium top-up, it is still entirely optional. However, please note 
that the additional top-up amount is recommended to improve the policy sustainability. Performing 
top-up from time to time will help to ensure sufficient cash value for the deduction of the relevant 
charges, fees and taxes. The policy might lapse earlier than expected if there is insufficient cash value 
to bear the cost for any further deduction.   
 
63. Can a customer choose to disable the Extension of Coverage Term feature?  
Yes. Customer can request to disable at any time after the policy is issued and prior to the Extension 
of Coverage Term, at no additional charges, by submitting the following documents which could be 
obtained from the sales representatives or any Prudential branches: 
• 
A completed Supplementary Proposal Form (SPF) when perform positive endorsement or 
Alteration Request Form (ARF) when perform negative endorsement 
• 
Acknowledged Policy Alteration Sustainability Statement (PASS) 
• 
Quotation Slip 
In such cases, the coverage will end at the original expiry date of the policy without the extension. 
 
64. If a customer changes his mind subsequently after disabling the Extension of Coverage Term, can 
customer re-enable for this feature?  
Yes. Customer can re-enable this feature prior to the Extension of Coverage Term by submitting the 
same set of documents as described in Q60. 
 
This application will be subject to the conditions set out in the policy which includes the requirement 
for re-underwriting. For example, the insured life must be in good health, and within the allowable age 
limit, otherwise this may result in higher insurance charges and/or premium required or the 
application may be rejected.

LAPSE AND REVIVAL 
96. When will the policy lapse? 
The policy will not lapse if the No-Lapse Provision is still in force. Otherwise, the policy will lapse when 
there is insufficient cash value in the policy accounts to cover for all charges. However, the policy, will 
be automatically revived if premium is received within 7 days from the lapsed date. 
 
97. What is No-Lapse Provision?  
The policy is guaranteed to in force within the first 6 years (72 months) of the policy regardless of the 
cash values in the policy, provided all premiums due are paid and no withdrawal from BUA is 
performed.  
 
 
43 
 
Any unpaid charges during the period when No-Lapse Provision is in effect (“Outstanding Charges”) 
are regarded as monies owing to us. We shall deduct the Outstanding Charges or part of the 
Outstanding Charges: 
i. by canceling units in the accounts at the unit price at the time of such cancellation when there are 
available units in the policy; 
ii. from any benefits or monies payable under the policy; 
iii. from any surrender value payable under this policy; or 
iv. from any amount payable as a result of redemption of units from the accounts of the policy. 
 
98. Is there any terms & conditions applicable to No-Lapse Provision? 
No-Lapse provision will be revoked if: 
(a) Premium is not paid up to date; or 
(b) Withdrawal has been made from the BUA.

to premium not paid up to date provided that : 
(a) Customer has paid all premium due when policy is still in force  
(b) Policy is revived and customer has paid all premium due if policy has already lapsed.

If the total cash value in BUA and IUA (if any) falls below zero after the 6th policy years (or the 73rd

TOTAL MULTI CRISIS CARE 
104. What is Total Multi Crisis Care?  
Total Multi Crisis Care is a comprehensive critical illness rider. It allows multiple claims on covered 
early stage critical illness (consist of early stage and intermediate stage) to late stage critical illnesses 
for up to 400% of the rider Sum Assured. 
 
 
105. What is the rider term for Total Multi Crisis Care?  
Rider term of Total Multi Crisis Care will follow the policy term. 
 
106. Is Total Multi Crisis Care available for all payment options under PRUWealth Enrich 2.0?  
No. Total Multi Crisis Care is only available for 10-pay, 20-pay, and full-pay options. It is not available 
for 5-pay. 
 
107. Why is Total Multi Crisis Care not available for 5-pay? 
• 
Premium Consideration: Attaching Total Multi Crisis Care with 5-pay structure would make 
premiums significantly higher. 
• 
Risk Management: Early critical illness coverage carries higher risk. Limiting to longer premium 
payment terms helps prevent situations where additional premiums might need to be charged 
after the 5-year payment term ends. 
• 
Customer experience: Avoids unexpected premium adjustments after the 5-year term, which 
could lead to dissatisfaction or lapses. 
 
108. What are the benefits does Total Multi Crisis Care offer?  
Early Critical Illness Coverage 
Early detection and diagnosis of critical illnesses improve the chances of successful treatment. Total 
Multi Crisis Care provides coverage on 101 early critical illness conditions (covers both early stage & 
intermediate stage) that pays 50% of the rider Sum Assured upon diagnosis of the covered condition. 
 
Critical Illness Coverage 
When someone is diagnosed with a life-threatening illness, the financial needs are greater than just 
the usual treatment cost. 100% of the rider Sum Assured will be payable upon diagnosis of the covered 
late stage critical illness. 
 
45 
 
 
Multiple Claims up to 400% of the Rider Sum Assured 
Multiple claims are allowed on different stages (if applicable to the rider) of the critical illness category 
with maximum of 100% of the rider Sum Assured for each critical illness category except for Cancer, 
Heart Attack, and Stroke which can be claimed twice. Maximum of 4 early critical illness claims are 
allowed subject to RM350,000 per claim limit. 
 
Re-diagnosis of Cancer, Heart Attack and Stroke Coverage 
Cancer, Heart Attack and Stroke are the most prevalent critical illnesses in Malaysia. The rider also 
pays benefit when one is diagnosed with subsequent Cancer, Heart Attack and Stroke before age 85, 
when it is unrelated to the first diagnosis. This means that rider covers up to 200% of the rider Sum 
Assured for critical illness category of Cancer, Heart Attack and Stroke. 
 
Additional Coverage for Special Benefit 
20% of the rider Sum Assured is payable upon diagnosis of any one of the covered Diabetic Related 
Conditions and Joint Related Conditions. This Special Benefit is payable once and will not reduce the 
rider Sum Assured.
